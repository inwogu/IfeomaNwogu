Dr. Ifeoma Nwogu


                University at Buffalo

I am an Associate Professor at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering and Co-Director of Graduate Studies. I have worked at the Center for Unified Biometrics and Sensors (CUBS) and the Center of Excellence for Document Analysis and Recogntion (CEDAR), since October 2011. The centers are affilliated with the Computer Science and Engineering department at the University at Buffalo (UB), also known as SUNY Buffalo. I completed my PhD in 2009 at UB, and was an NSF-sponsored postdoctoral researcher at the Computer Science department at the University of Rochester, working with Dr. Chris Brown in Computer Vision. Before that, I completed my master's degree in Computer and Information Science at the University of Pennsylvania and my Electrical Engineering undergraduate degree at the University of Lagos, Nigeria.

My current research interests are in computational social psychology, primarily in developing dynamic statistical modeling to study different forms of human behaviors such as deception detection.


I am looking for motivated students to join my research lab HBML(Human Behaviroal Modelling Lab) at UB. If you are interested in working working with me, please send your CV and a brief description of your past experiences and projects relevant to my areas of interest at inwogu@buffalo.edu.
